{%trans "Summary" %}

{% blocktrans %} The goal of this project is to collect as many recordings from native Mandarin Chinese speakers. These recordings will be used to help students learning Mandarin Chinese practice their tone recognition. {% endblocktrans %}

{%trans "Practical" %}

{%trans "Registration" %}

{% blocktrans %} You will need to register on the registration page and fill in your information.
Your name will only be used for attribution, If you do not want to give your real name you can just fill in an alias.
If you do not fill in any name your e-mail address will be used for attribution.
If you want to participate in this project you will also need accept that your recordings will be released under the Creative Commons CC BY-SA 4.0 license, click on the link to find more information about the license. {% endblocktrans %}

{%trans "Recording" %}

{% blocktrans %} After registration you can go the recording page. There you will be shown pinyin you can record using the record button. Click on the stop button when you finish recording and press the play button to listen to your recording. Restart your recording if necessary or click on the upload button to upload your recording. Once the recording is uploaded a new pinyin word to be recorded will appear.
You can do as many recordings as you like, and come back any time to the recording page to do more recordings.
here is an example of some pinyin and the recordings: {% endblocktrans %}

  • {% trans "mā as in 妈 (first tone)" %}

  • {% trans "má as in 麻 (second tone)" %}

  • {% trans "mǎ as in 马 (third tone)" %}

  • {% trans "mà as in 骂 (forth tone)" %}

  • {% trans "ma as in 吗 (fifth/zeroth/no tone)" %}

    •

    {% blocktrans %} If you record multiple syllables at once, for example: "mā rì pǐng", try to pronounce them as if it was one word. Any combination that has a meaning is pure coincidence. {% endblocktrans %}
